Nikko J. Cleri

Eberly Postdoctoral Fellow at Penn State

I study the evolution of galaxies across cosmic time through emission-line spectroscopy. I primarily use observations from JWST as a member of the CEERS, NGDEEP, and RUBIES collaborations. I am currently most interested in studying sources of ionization in galaxies in early-Universe galaxies.

I am also very active in outreach and education in both academic and public audiences. I am dedicated to making physics and astronomy accessible and interesting to people of all ages and backgrounds.

On this website you can find information about several of my past and present research projects and outreach work.
