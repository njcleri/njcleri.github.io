Nikko Cleri He/Him/His

Eberly Postdoctoral Fellow

The Pennsylvania State University

I study the evolution of galaxies across cosmic time. I am privileged to use exquisite spectroscopy from JWST as a member of the CEERS, NGDEEP, and RUBIES collaborations. I am interested in using spectra to study the underlying physics of galaxies, from the sources of their ionizing photons to the chemical abundances of their stars and gas.

I am also very active in mentoring, outreach and education in both academic and general audiences. My goal is to make physics and astronomy accessible and interesting to people of all ages and backgrounds.

On this website you can find information about my research and outreach work, as well as a compilation of several potentially useful resources. Feel free to get in touch!
